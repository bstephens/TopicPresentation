Introduction

 

Process Abstraction

  • Central concept in all programming languages
  • Babbage's Analytical Engine (1840s) able to reuse collections of instruction cards at several different places in a program
  • This collection of statements is written as a subprogram in modern programming languages
  • Reuse saves memory space and coding time
  • Increases readability of a program by exposing the logical structure while hiding low-level details

Data Abstraction

  • Originally considered less important than process abstraction until the 1980s
  • Recognizing and focusing on important characteristics of an object or situation
  • Discussed more in chapter 11


Subprograms are closely related to the methods of object-oriented languages, but they differ in the way they are called and their associations with classes and objects.  Shared features, such as parameters and local variables are discussed in this chapter, but special characteristics of methods are described in Chapter 12.
